About the Summit

This landmark summit will convene policymakers, industry leaders, and global experts to explore the theme: “The Potential of Small Modular and Micro Reactors in Accelerating Africa’s Energy Transition.”

We will spotlight the transformative role of nuclear innovation, with a particular emphasis on Small Modular and Micro Reactors (SMRs and MMRs) and the enabling conditions necessary for their success—infrastructure, financing, political will, and localized technology development.

The summit is organized in collaboration with key international institutions, including International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA), Nuclear Energy Agency (NEA), World Nuclear Association (WNA) , and leading regional financial institutions.

Why are we convening?

Addressing Energy Poverty: Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and Micro Reactors (MRs) offer scalable, low-carbon solutions to expand energy access in remote and underserved areas.

Supporting Industrialization: Reliable power from SMRs/MRs can support critical sectors like Mining, Manufacturing, Healthcare, and Education, Powering Data Centers for Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Policy and Investment Opportunities

Regulatory Frameworks

Understand evolving nuclear policies, safety protocols, and licensing requirements in African contexts.

Attracting Capital

Discover how SMRs and MRs are attracting public and private investment, and the financing models that work in emerging markets.

Technological Innovation & Knowledge Sharing

Latest Advances

Learn about cutting-edge reactor technologies, deployment models, and international best practices.

R&D CollaborationA

Connect with innovators and researchers to foster partnerships on nuclear development tailored to Africa's unique needs.

Energy Mix for a Changing Climate

Decarbonization Goals

Explore how SMRs and MRs can complement renewables to reduce emissions and achieve net-zero targets..

Energy Resilience

SMRs/MRs offer resilient, on-demand energy with minimal environmental footprint—ideal for off-grid or disaster-prone areas.

Sustainable Strategic Partnerships

Stakeholder Engagement

Collaborate with energy ministers, utility executives, developers, regulators, financiers, and international experts.

Form Collaborations

Build partnerships that can accelerate national and regional nuclear projects.
