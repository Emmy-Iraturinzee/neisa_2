About the Summit

This landmark summit will convene policymakers, industry leaders, and global experts to explore the theme: “The Potential of Small Modular and Micro Reactors in Accelerating Africa’s Energy Transition.”

We will spotlight the transformative role of nuclear innovation, with a particular emphasis on Small Modular and Micro Reactors (SMRs and MMRs) and the enabling conditions necessary for their success—infrastructure, financing, political will, and localized technology development.

The summit is organized in collaboration with key international institutions, including International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA), Nuclear Energy Agency (NEA), World Nuclear Association (WNA) , and leading regional financial institutions.

Why are we convening?

Addressing Energy Poverty: Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and Micro Reactors (MRs) offer scalable, low-carbon solutions to expand energy access in remote and underserved areas.

Supporting Industrialization: Reliable power from SMRs/MRs can support critical sectors like Mining, Manufacturing, Healthcare, and Education, Powering Data Centers for Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Who Should Attend & Why?

Government & Policy Makers
  • Understand how SMRs & MMRs can fast-track electrification & energy demands, especially in rural and industrial zones.
  • Learn how to align national policies with global nuclear innovation and safety standards.
  • Explore options for public-private partnerships and regional power pooling.
Nuclear Regulatory Authorities
  • Contribute to policy dialogues and thought leadership on Africa’s clean energy transition.
  • Support capacity building initiatives and cross-border cooperation.
  • Shape regional strategies and share international best practices.
Energy Utilities & Infrastructure Developers
  • Collaborate to develop SMR/MMR deployment models to meet Africa’s decentralized energy needs.
  • Engage with technology vendors and financiers offering turnkey and scalable solutions.
  • Gain insight into grid integration, licensing, and site selection.
Scientific & Technical Experts
  • Deep dive into latest SMR/MMR technologies, safety systems, and design innovations.
  • Exchange knowledge on waste management, fuel cycles, and localization of R&D.
  • Form research partnerships with global institutions and African educational institutions.
Investors and Finance Professionals
  • Identify bankable projects and understand risk-mitigation frameworks around nuclear innovation in Africa.
  • Explore funding models, blended finance, and green bond potential for SMRs.
  • Meet early-stage innovators and project developers seeking investment.
Energy Users (Mining, Manufacturing, Data Centers)
  • Harness SMRs as reliable off-grid power to support industrial growth.
  • Explore custom energy solutions for mining, logistics, and agro-processing.
  • Evaluate cost and ROI comparisons with diesel, solar, and grid extensions.
Civil Society
  • Be at the forefront of a transformative energy narrative for Africa.
  • Learn about SMRs/MMRs and their role in Africa’s energy future.
  • Understand safety, transparency, and regulatory measures.
R&D Institutions and Universities
  • Connect with nuclear education and workforce development initiatives.
  • Gain access to curriculum resources, internships, and exchange programs.
  • Position your institution in regional R&D and capacity-development partnerships.

Spotlight: Innovations Powering the Future

This session will spotlight breakthrough innovations in Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) and their transformative potential for Africa’s energy landscape. Industry leaders and technology developers will present the latest advancements in SMR design, deployment, and scalability. Discussions will highlight how these innovations can provide reliable, flexible, and low-carbon energy solutions tailored to Africa’s growing and decentralized energy needs.

Infrastructure Readiness for SMRs and Micro Reactors in Africa

The successful deployment of Small Modular and Micro Reactors depends on more than technology—it requires robust supporting infrastructure. This session will assess Africa’s current infrastructure landscape and identify critical gaps in grid capacity, transportation, water access, and skilled workforce development. Experts will explore what it takes to create enabling conditions for SMR/MMR rollout, including integrated planning, cross-sector coordination, and investment in local capabilities.

Knowledge Nexus: Advancement in Fuel Types & Waste Management Innovation

As nuclear technologies evolve, so too must the approaches to fuel design and waste management. This session will explore cutting-edge developments in advanced fuel types, including those tailored for SMRs and Micro Reactors, as well as innovative solutions for managing nuclear waste safely and sustainably. Experts will discuss circular fuel cycles, storage innovations, and the potential for regional collaboration in addressing Africa’s unique needs and future readiness.

Spotlight: Women Championing Nuclear Energy Implementation in Africa

From engineering and policy to advocacy and education, female leaders are shaping the continent’s nuclear future. Panelists will share their journeys, challenges, and impact—while discussing strategies to increase gender inclusion, mentorship, and leadership in the nuclear sector. The session aims to amplify women's voices and accelerate a more diverse and equitable energy transition.
